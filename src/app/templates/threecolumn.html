About the Model
CitySense is a natural language processing project that uses online travel reviews, articles, and blog posts about cities in Europe.

Business Question

The problem that I’m interested in solving is how to discern the personality of a city, based on first person reviews and blog posts that are available online. It’s pretty easy to find out the best places to stay or eat in a new city, but it can be hard to know what the ambiance will be like, or how you’ll feel when you’re actually there.

Data Preparation

For this project, I focused on doing natural language processing over trip advisor reviews and Rick Steves blog posts and articles. I scraped 72,000 total reviews and posts for about 200 cities in Europe, and then went through many iterations of exploratory data analysis, trying different stop words criteria, and KMeans clustering on my data. I used scikit-learn’s TfidfVectorizer and cosine similarity to create my model, and then deployed it as a web app.

Modeling

In order to distill the corpus down to the most useful words I tagged each word by its part of speech and then decided on upper and lower bounding criteria for the most common parts of speech in terms of both document frequency and term frequency. Next, I chose 15 different personalities based on what I was seeing in my text and leveraging traits that showed up in personality profiles such as Myers Briggs. The text were vectorized using scikit-learn's TfidfVectorizer, and then I used cosine similarity to find the cities that best matched the different personality types.

Evaluation

There isn't a straightforward mathematical way to evaluate how well my model is doing. I created a survey including a subset of the cities in my dataset and used that to get feedback from people who had been to those cities. I'm continually trying to improve my model, so I welcome any and all feedback.
